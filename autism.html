What is Autism?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects communication, behavior, and social interactions. It is called a "spectrum" because it presents differently in each individual, ranging from mild to severe challenges.

Common Symptoms

  • Difficulty with social interactions
  • Repetitive behaviors and interests
  • Highly focused interests
  • Sensory sensitivities (e.g., to light, sound, or textures)
  • Delayed or unique speech patterns

Causes and Diagnosis

The exact cause of autism is unknown, but research suggests a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Early diagnosis can be made by medical professionals through behavioral observations and standardized assessments.

Myths vs. Facts

  • Myth: Autism is caused by vaccines.
    Fact: Scientific studies have debunked this claim.
  • Myth: People with autism lack emotions.
    Fact: They experience emotions but may express them differently.
  • Myth: Autism is a disease.
    Fact: It is a neurological difference, not an illness.
