🚀 The ISS (International Space Station)

408 km above Earth. The largest human-made structure in space. You could fit a football field on it!

🌌 Low Earth Orbit (LEO)

Where most satellites live. This is also where space tourism flights start and end!

🔭 Hubble Space Telescope

At 547 km, the Hubble has been capturing stunning cosmic imagery since 1990.

🌍 Kármán Line

100 km above sea level. It’s where space begins! Below this, aerodynamic lift matters. Above this, rockets rule.

✨ Space Weather

Earth’s magnetic field protects us from solar winds. Without it, we’d be toast – literally!

☄️ Meteoroid Zone

Ever wonder about shooting stars? They’re meteoroids burning up as they enter the atmosphere!

🌈 Aurora Borealis

The magical Northern Lights occur between 80-640 km above Earth due to charged solar particles.

🚀 First Human in Space

Yuri Gagarin, in 1961, reached 327 km and became the first human to orbit Earth. "I see Earth. It's so beautiful."

🌪 Troposphere

Up to 20 km high, this is where all weather happens. Clouds, storms, and turbulence – welcome to the party!

🦅 Highest Bird Flight

The Rüppell's griffon vulture reached 11.3 km. It had a close encounter with an airplane – yikes!

🏔 Mount Everest

At 8.8 km, it’s Earth’s tallest mountain. Yet, it’s only a fraction of our journey!

🚁 Helicopter Record

The highest helicopter flight reached 12.4 km in 1972. Beyond this, air gets too thin for blades to work!

🏢 Burj Khalifa

The tallest building in the world (828 m). If you dropped a penny off the top, it’d fall for 10 seconds!

🏊 Challenger Deep

The deepest part of the ocean is 10.9 km down. It’s deeper than Mount Everest is tall!

🌬 Felix Baumgartner's Jump

In 2012, Felix jumped from 39 km, breaking the sound barrier during freefall. Gravity works hard!

🐘 Animal Records

The bar-headed goose can fly over the Himalayas, reaching 7.2 km. Imagine the view!

🚢 Titanic Depth

The Titanic rests at a depth of 3.8 km in the North Atlantic Ocean. A chilling end to a grand ship.

🌋 Mauna Loa

The largest volcano on Earth, Mauna Loa, rises 4.1 km above sea level but extends far deeper underwater.

🌍 Earth's Surface

You’ve made it back! Time to celebrate the sheer awesomeness of our planet. 🌎
